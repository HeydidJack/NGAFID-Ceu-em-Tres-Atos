NGAFID (National General Aviation Flight Information Database). This dataset serves as the primary benchmark for real-world whole-aircraft health diagnosis research, comprising multivariate time-series data collected from a Cessna-172 fleet operated by a real flight school. Unlike idealized simulation environments such as C-MAPSS, NGAFID encompasses over 28,000 flights accumulating 31,000 flight hours, correlated with more than 2,000 unscheduled maintenance events spanning 36 distinct maintenance categories. The dataset captures 23-dimensional sensor measurements sampled at 1 Hz, covering critical subsystems including electrical systems (voltage/current), fuel systems (fuel quantities and flow), engine parameters (oil temperature/pressure, RPM), cylinder status (CHT/EGT), flight status (IAS, vertical speed, altitude), and environmental parameters (OAT).
NGAFID exhibits three fundamental characteristics that distinguish it from synthetic benchmarks: Authenticity, reflecting genuine environmental noise, operational variability, and label ambiguity inherent in real maintenance records; Target Component Sparsity, where fault-related discriminative features concentrate in the 12th-18th principal components contributing less than 0.1% of total variance, rendering fault signatures susceptible to masking by high-dimensional operational noise; and Multi-Stage Coupling, where flight phases (taxi, takeoff/climb, cruise, descent/approach) exhibit statistically heterogeneous dynamics with strong temporal dependencies that prohibit phase-wise isolation.
The dataset provides two standardized configurations: the benchmark Subset (19 classes, 11,446 flights) for controlled validation, and the complete Overall dataset (36 classes, 28,935 flights) for open-environment evaluation. Both exhibit severe dual imbalance: quantitative disparity between healthy and anomalous samples, and extreme long-tailed fault distributions spanning three orders of magnitude (head classes >2,000 flights versus tail classes <15). This reflects objective fleet reality where chronic wear predominates over acute failures, rather than sampling bias. Preprocessing involves forward-fill for missing values, cubic spline interpolation to fixed length 2,048 points, and stratified 5-fold cross-validation with strict physical isolation between folds to prevent data leakage.
