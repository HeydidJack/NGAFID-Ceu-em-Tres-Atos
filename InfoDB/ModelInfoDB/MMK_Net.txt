MMK Net (Multi-Micro Kernel Network). This model serves as the specialized local feature extractor for the Micro-stage Fault Classification task within the Diagnosis Decomposition Framework. Architecturally designed to address the receptive field paradox in aircraft health diagnosis, MMK Net employs a multi-scale micro-convolution strategy that strictly restricts the receptive field to isolate fault-related local temporal segments while suppressing cross-stage operational noise. Each MMK Block consists of a bottleneck 1x1 convolution for dimensionality reduction, followed by parallel 1D convolution branches with kernel sizes of 1, 3, and 5 to capture multi-granularity fault patterns across different temporal scales. The outputs of these parallel branches are concatenated along the channel dimension and subsequently passed through Layer Normalization and ReLU activation. Notably, the architecture deliberately omits pooling layers to preserve temporal resolution, and adopts LayerNorm instead of BatchNorm to stabilize feature scales under severe class imbalance conditions where batch statistics would be distorted by dominant healthy samples. Residual connections are applied every three blocks to mitigate gradient vanishing while maintaining shallow feature sharpness. With maximum kernel size limited to 5 and no global aggregation mechanisms, MMK Net ensures that the receptive field remains significantly smaller than the full sequence length, enabling precise extraction of microscopic fault signatures that would otherwise be drowned by global operational heterogeneity in monolithic end-to-end architectures.
