ConvTok-MHSA (Multi-Head Self-Attention with Global Receptive Field). This model implements a pure global attention mechanism atop the ConvTok tokenization framework, serving as the primary baseline for whole-profile context aggregation. The architecture first segments the input multivariate time series into non-overlapping patches via ConvTok, which encodes local shape descriptors through 1-D CNN, augments tokens with patch-wise mean and standard deviation statistics, and injects sinusoidal positional encodings. These rich tokens are then processed by a standard Multi-Head Self-Attention (MHSA) backbone without propagation constraints, allowing every token to attend to every other token across the entire flight profile. While this global aggregation captures long-range operational dependencies and achieves superior performance on Anomaly Detection (79.29% accuracy, confirming that whole-profile context is indispensable for binary health assessment), it suffers from noise injection in Fault Classification tasks where cross-stage operational variations dilute weak local fault signatures.
