LMSD (Long-Micro Scale Diagnostician). As the concrete architectural instantiation of the Diagnosis Decomposition Framework, LMSD explicitly decouples the end-to-end diagnosis task into two heterogeneous stages connected by a hard-threshold routing mechanism. The Long Stage employs ConvTokMHSA with global receptive fields to perform Anomaly Detection on the complete dataset, capturing cross-temporal operational contexts and distinguishing systematic deviations from normal flight envelopes. The Micro Stage utilizes MMK Net with strictly restricted receptive fields to conduct Fault Classification exclusively on samples flagged as anomalous, focusing on local microscopic features without interference from global operational patterns. The Hard-Threshold Routing mechanism enforces dimensional isolation between stages through negative infinity masking: healthy samples trigger the output vector construction with dimension 0 populated and remaining dimensions set to negative infinity, completely bypassing the Fault Analyzer, while anomalous samples route to the Micro Stage with dimension 0 masked out, ensuring that fault probability distributions remain uninfluenced by the healthy category. The Decoupled Training Strategy physically isolates the optimization pathways through gradient hard-isolation: the Health Analyzer trains on the full dataset with large-sample lightweight configuration, while the Fault Analyzer trains exclusively on the anomalous subset with small-sample complex settings, preventing fine-grained gradients from interfering with global pattern learning. This architectural separation fundamentally resolves the receptive field conflict inherent in monolithic end-to-end models, addresses class imbalance by eliminating healthy sample contamination in the fault discrimination space, and achieves superior training efficiency through intelligent allocation of computational resources between large-sample lightweight and small-sample complex optimization pathways.
