ConvTok-LPLA (Log-Parse Local Attention). This hybrid architecture strikes a balance between local discrimination and global context by combining restricted-window heads with selective global attention and log-parse sparsification. The configuration assigns narrow local windows to most attention heads for noise-suppressed local feature extraction, while reserving one or more heads with w=0 (global receptive field) to capture long-range dependencies. Crucially, these global heads undergo Log-Parse sparsification followed by Sparsemax normalization, which retains only the top-k attention weights and blocks the re-introduction of irrelevant long-range noise. LPLA thus offers a compromise solution for scenarios requiring both local fault signature detection and limited global operational context, though experimental results indicate that even this constrained global interaction slightly underperforms strictly local variants (SWLA/MWLA) in high-noise fault classification scenarios.
