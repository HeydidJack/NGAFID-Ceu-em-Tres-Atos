InceptionTime (Multi-Scale Deep Convolutional Architecture). Inspired by the Inception modules originally developed for computer vision, this deep convolutional model captures temporal patterns at multiple scales without relying on recurrent connections. Each Inception block consists of a bottleneck 1x1 convolution for dimensionality reduction, followed by four parallel branches: three convolutional filters with kernel sizes 10, 20, and 40 to extract multi-resolution temporal features, and a max-pooling branch with an additional 1x1 projection. The four branch outputs, each containing filters channels, are concatenated along the channel dimension to produce 4*filters features, followed by batch normalization and ReLU activation. To facilitate training in deep configurations, residual connections are implemented every 3 layers, adding the input of the block to its output. After stacking multiple Inception blocks (default depth of 4), global average pooling collapses the temporal dimension, and a final linear layer maps to the classification space. This architecture efficiently balances local and semi-global receptive fields, providing a strong convolutional baseline that is more expressive than simple CNNs while avoiding the sequential computation bottleneck of recurrent models.
