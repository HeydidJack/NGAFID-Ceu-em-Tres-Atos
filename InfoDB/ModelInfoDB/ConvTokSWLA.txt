ConvTok-SWLA (Sliding Window Local Attention). This variant addresses the signal-to-noise ratio degradation inherent in global attention by enforcing a strictly local receptive field through a fixed-width sliding window mask. After ConvTok tokenization, the self-attention mechanism is constrained by a learnable binary band mask where each token can only attend to neighbors within a fixed half-width window w, effectively zeroing attention weights for distant tokens. This hard locality constraint forces the model to focus exclusively on neighborhood patterns and suppresses long-range operational noise that is irrelevant to specific fault manifestations. Empirical results demonstrate that SWLA significantly outperforms global attention in Fault Classification (F1 improvements of +17-19 percentage points), validating that restricting information propagation to local contexts recovers weak fault signatures buried in high-noise operational profiles.
